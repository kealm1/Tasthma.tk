Consult
Your GP
If you think you may have symptoms of asthma, for example if you wheeze or cough with your hay fever, then talk to your GP about determining whether they could be related to asthma or not.
Learn
Asthma First Aid
Learn the 4 steps of asthma first aid. If you develop asthma symptoms, follow the four steps of asthma first aid and make sure you follow up with your GP.
Track
Thunderstorm Activity
Be aware of thunderstorm asthma forecasts during pollen season.
Create
Asthma Action Plan
Make sure you have a written asthma action plan that includes thunderstorm advice - talk to your GP.
Purchase
Prevention Medication
Have reliever medication appropriately available in grass pollen season and be aware of how to use it, ideally with a spacer.
Choose to
Stay Indoors
For those at risk, where possible it’s best to avoid being outside during thunderstorms during the grass pollen season – especially in the wind gusts that come before the storm. Go inside and close your doors and windows and if you have your air conditioner on, turn it to recirculate.
