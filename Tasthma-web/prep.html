Consult
Your GP

If you believe you may have experienced symptoms of asthma, especially wheezing or coughing with your hay fever, see your GP today about establishing whether these symptoms could be connected to asthma.

Learn
Asthma First Aid

Gain a practical knowledge of the 4 steps of asthma first aid. These can be useful if you develop asthma symptoms.

Observe the four steps of asthma first aid and remember to discuss this further with your GP.

Track
Thunderstorm Activity

Be aware of thunderstorm asthma forecasts during pollen season.

Sign-up for risk alerts on www.tasthma.tk.

Create
Asthma Action Plan

Talk to your GP to develop a written asthma action plan that includes thunderstorm advice, created to suit your individual needs.

Purchase
Prevention Medication

Discuss reliever medication options with your pharmacist or GP.

Make sure it is within easy access during the grass pollen season (Oct-Dec) and be informed of how it must be consumed.

Choose to
Stay Indoors

Where possible, it is wise to avoid being outside during thunderstorms, throughout the grass pollen season (Oct-Dec). Avoid the wind gusts that precede a thunderstorm.

Stay indoors and close your doors and windows. If you are using an air conditioner, set it to recirculate.

Be alert to
Asthma Symptoms

Be attentive to and take measures on the development of asthma symptoms.

Follow the steps described in your asthma action plan.
